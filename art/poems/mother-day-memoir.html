mother's day memoir

her two feet crossed oceans her two hands drove a boat against Pacific her mind is travelling through six dimensions her heart is beating loud as Chopin’s classic courage is not given, but imprinted resilience is not built, but earned independence is not seek, but constructed but her love and warmth are taught no man can define, she won’t let them no man can destroy, she won’t buckle no man can possess, she owns herself no man can trespass, she guards the gate the crown is for her mother all hail to the laborer she holds dearly closer until the God takes her
